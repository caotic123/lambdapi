The proposal: make the cubical primitives conversion, not encoding
The decision the file makes is to stop trying to fit the hard layers into confluent rewriting and instead extend Lambdapi's conversion itself so the primitives are decided. The two new constructs are not ad hoc — each lands on exactly one of the walls from the analysis, and named in your own vocabulary they are familiar machinery.
theory is CC(X) for Lambdapi. The block theory Interval { carrier ≔ I; canon ≔ can; value ≔ Dnf; equal ≔ eqDnf; consistent ≔ wf; solve ≔ none } is a Shostak-style theory registered as the X of a congruence-closure-modulo-X, in the Conchon–Contejean sense (the architecture under Alt-Ergo). can is the canonizer, Dnf is the value domain (the "(X)"), gen is the alien-abstraction map at the CC↔X leaf, eqDnf is equality-in-X, wf is the consistency check that detects ⊥ = ⊤ (the i0 = i1 collapse), and the comment's rebuild is literally egg/egglog's re-canonization after merge. The payoff is precisely the resolution of wall #1: the AC-matching that strained stock Dedukti is gone. You don't rewrite modulo associativity/commutativity/idempotence/absorption and pray for confluence — you interpret into a canonical value where i ∧ j ≡ j ∧ i is free because the DNF is a set. The solve ≔ none is the honest part and exactly right: the free de Morgan algebra has a canonizer but no Shostak solver (you cannot isolate a variable from i ∧ j = i0), so it integrates canonizer-only. And the assertnot lines guard the one law that must not hold — i ∨ ~i ≢ i1, de Morgan and not Boolean — which means negD must keep i ∨ ~i irreducible, the single most error-prone point in the whole canonizer.
when is type-directed, saturating constraint. when p : Path T u v ⊢ p @ i0 ≡ u is the move the prior turn said the cofibration layer needs and untyped conversion cannot give: the guard reads the type of p, so conversion becomes type-directed exactly where it must be, and nowhere else. Two consequences follow. The endpoints are unannotated — u, v come from the guard, not from a CCHM-style @u0,u1 annotation on the term — which is strictly nicer than Agda's elaboration. And path-η (pabs T (λ i, p @ i) ≡ p) works because the e-graph saturates an equivalence instead of orienting a rewrite — p and ⟨i⟩(p @ i) cohabit one class. The file's claim that this "cannot be a confluent rewrite in stock Lambdapi" is right for the precise reason that η-contraction on paths is fragile against the boundary rules under untyped orientation; saturation sidesteps the critical pairs the way Agda's typed definitional η does. This is your egglog work load-bearing inside the kernel.
They compose downward, and the composition is where the wall actually falls. There is a clean dependency tower: the plain Dnf functions, then the Interval/Face theories over them, then ⊨ defined by reducing through the Face canonizer (φ ⊨ ψ ↪ eqDnf (conjD (canF φ)(canF ψ)) (canF φ) = true, i.e. φ ⊓ ψ = φ, lattice order), then when φ ⊨ f1 ⊢ comp … ≡ sys i1 whose guard calls the decision procedure. The discipline stated in the comments — face formula in a guard is fine, face formula in a head is a category error — is not a stylistic note, it is the soundness boundary. Consuming a disjunction-decision in a guard is or-elimination (conjunctive, decidable). Producing a value over a disjunction in a head would be primPOr-with-coherence, the genuinely hard thing. The framework lets you consume the join and refuses to let you construct over it carelessly.
Ledgered against the three walls

De Morgan interval algebra — solved, by canonization into Dnf. The assert/assertnot battery (absorption, involution, distributivity, no-excluded-middle) is exactly the conformance test, and it holds by ≡.
Cofibration entailment / face lattice — solved for the entailment half. ⊨ is decided by the Face theory (note canF = can plus only Fdiscr, so opposite faces are disjoint), and entailment is now usable as a side-condition, not a SAT call bolted on.
Path / boundary / η — solved and now uniform with the above: β is a plain rewrite, boundary and η are when constraints, all decided by one conversion.
Partial-element coherence + Kan operations — mechanism present, demonstration absent. comp exists and the collapse case (φ ⊨ f1) fires, but the general hcomp/comp filling and primPOr gluing of partial elements that must agree on overlaps is not in the file. The mechanism plausibly reaches it — a when (φ ⊓ ψ) ⊨ … ⊢ u ≡ v whose guard is the overlap entailment and whose head is the agreement equation — but it is not delivered.

That last line is the real result of the proposal: the three separate walls of the analysis collapse to one open question — **on ground terms** e-graph saturation of overlap-agreement constraints terminates and leaves conversion decidable (finite ground congruence closure); the genuinely open case is when the constraints carry **metavariables**, where saturation interleaves with unification. Everything else is dispatched. (See "Design conclusion" below for the ground/metavariable split.)
What the thesis then owes
The framework relocates the hard equalities into a setting (CC(X) + egglog saturation + type-directed guards) where each is individually decidable; the theorem to prove is that the combined conversion is still (i) sound — contained in the intended CCHM definitional equality, so adding when/theory doesn't equate distinct types and break consistency (this is where consistent ≔ wf does its work, but it must be proven sufficient); (ii) decidable, with saturation reaching a fixpoint; and (iii) a congruence. The standing cost to name explicitly: typed conversion threads type information through the kernel and interacts with metavariables/unification, which stock Dedukti's untyped conversion avoids.
And the framing your advisor's "motivate the claims" note wants: cubical is the stress test that justifies λΠ-SMT. It is the case where plain rewriting provably is not enough, so decision-procedure-extended conversion is not a convenience but a necessity — and the theory block is exactly a cvc5/Alt-Ergo-style procedure (with can as RARE-flavored canon rules) registered into the kernel, with a clear path to emitting Alethe-style certificates for each CC(X) merge.
Frontier and next step
The one place I'd resist overclaiming is IsOne: the file bypasses proof-irrelevance by working with F and ⊨ directly rather than IsOne r and witnesses. That is probably the right design, but it makes your faces a reformulation of Agda's, so the proposal owes a sentence on whether the reformulation is conservative — same equalities decided, nothing lost.
Concretely, two ways forward and I'd do either now: extend the file with the primPOr + overlap-agreement when and an hcomp reduction, so the one open question is exercised on a real example (the associativity-of-composition square is the canonical stressor) — or typeset the above as a thesis section, where the four-row ledger and the "three walls → one question" line are the spine. # Metatheory, sharpened

The framework's claims rest on obligations the analysis above bundles. Separated, the ledger reads as **one decidability question plus a soundness obligation `wf` does not discharge** — not "three walls → one question" flat. Four points.

**1. Soundness splits in two; `wf` covers only the first half.** (a) *X-theory consistency* — the registered theory must never equate `i0`/`i1` (or `f0`/`f1`); this is `consistent ≔ wf`, a guard on the **value domain** `Dnf`, and it is what keeps CC-modulo-X from deriving ⊥ = ⊤ by interval/face reasoning. (b) *`when`-rule validity* — each `when` head (boundary `p @ i0 ≡ u`, path-η, the hcomp/comp face laws, the primPOr restrictions, the overlap-agreement) must be a **valid CCHM definitional equality**. `wf` never inspects these rules; it sees only `Dnf`. So "(i) … this is where `consistent ≔ wf` does its work, but it must be proven sufficient" overstates: `wf` is *necessary* for (a) and *silent* on (b). (b) is a separate, by-hand containment ⟦when-rules⟧ ⊆ (CCHM ≡). Keep them apart, or the spine is contestable.

**2. The guard's left side is the ambient face context — and the sketch's total-face guard was backwards.** "On φ" means "the ambient face constraint Φ_Γ entails φ," i.e. the guard is `Φ_Γ ⊨ φ`, decided by the Face theory. The sketch wrote comp's collapse as `φ ⊨ f1`, but `φ ⊨ f1` ≡ `φ ≤ ⊤` is **trivially true**, so the collapse would fire unconditionally — unsound. It means `f1 ⊨ φ` (φ = ⊤), and in general `Φ_Γ ⊨ φ`. Under the corrected guard the demonstration `comp ((i=0)⊔(i=1)) … ≡ sys i1` no longer holds as a **closed** assert — `(i=0)⊔(i=1) ≠ f1` (no excluded middle on faces, mirroring `assertnot i ∨ ~i ≡ i1`); it holds only as a restriction in a context entailing that face. Both fixes are applied in `sketch.lp` (the `comp` guard and a `CAVEAT` on the demo). Pinning the guard direction is part of obligation 1(b).

**3. Decidability is coupled to unification through metavariables — that, not the cubical geometry, is where the open question bites.** To fire `when p : Path T u v ⊢ p @ i0 ≡ u` the kernel needs `p`'s type at conversion time, so conversion is type-directed *globally* (it must carry/synthesize enough type to dispatch), even though it only *uses* types in the path/face cases — the standing cost the proposal names. Sharper: when `u, v` or a system's faces carry **metavariables**, the boundary/overlap `when`s fire on undetermined terms, so saturation and unification interleave — termination must be proved against a **moving** set of e-classes (instantiation can add merges mid-saturation), not a fixed one. The associativity-of-composition square (`sketch.lp` §7c) exercises exactly this: the four corner agreements are decidable *given* the faces; the teeth are in the metavariable coupling, not the four-corner combinatorics.

**4. The IsOne reformulation is conservative (the owed sentence).** Working with `F`/`⊨` instead of `IsOne r` + witnesses loses nothing iff (i) `⊨` decides exactly the entailments `IsOne` proofs inhabit — `Φ ⊨ φ` ⟺ `IsOne φ` derivable under `IsOne Φ` — and (ii) the *only* role of an `IsOne` witness in CCHM definitional equality is to **license** a face-restricted equation, which is precisely what the `Φ_Γ ⊨ φ` guard supplies. (i) holds because `canF` normal forms decide free-de-Morgan face entailment; (ii) is why dropping proof-irrelevance changes the *presentation*, not the decided theory. Same equalities, witnesses elided.

**5. The ambient face context Φ_Γ is exactly Cubical Agda's Θ — it is not an ad-hoc kernel addition.** In Vezzosi–Mörtberg–Abel's case-tree judgment (ICFP 2019, §4.3 / Fig. 3) `Σ; Γ ⊢ f q̄ := Q : C | Θ ⤳ Σ′`, the list `Θ = α₁;…;αₙ` of *boundary assignments* is threaded through elaboration exactly as `Φ_Γ` is threaded through conversion here: `CtIntRoPath` pushes the endpoint faces `(i=0),(i=1)` onto `Θ`, and `CtDone` discharges every face of `Θ` by requiring `f q̄[αᵢ] = v[αᵢ]`. So `Φ_Γ` is their `Θ`, relocated from definition-time coverage to conversion-time guards — which both *justifies* the type-directed-conversion cost (point 3: the kernel threads `Θ`/`Φ_Γ` just as their elaborator already does) and pins the correspondence exactly: the §3 boundary `when` **is** `CtDone` for the faces `CtIntRoPath` pushed; the §5 overlap-agreement **is** `CtSplitPartial`'s "the `Qᵢ` must agree on the intersections"; the §6 hcomp face law **is** `CtSplitConHIT`'s `(r=i1)` extension. One difference cuts in our favour: `CtIntRoPath` carries an *annotated* path application `@u₀,u₁`, whereas our boundary `when` reads `u₀,u₁` off the type, so terms stay unannotated (proposal line 4, now concrete in `sketch.lp` PART III).

**Status.** `sketch.lp` Part II now implements partial elements, `⌊_⌋` forcing, `pcst`, `primPOr` with the two restriction `when`s **and** the overlap-agreement `when`, `hcomp` with its face laws, path composition and double composition via `hcomp`, the minimal four-corner square that makes overlap-agreement fire non-vacuously, and the associativity `assert` that *is* the open question made concrete. Part III represents the two case-tree rules `CtIntRoPath` and `CtDone` (Fig. 3), with the `Θ ≅ Φ_Γ` correspondence and a worked elaboration of path inversion `sym` whose two `CtDone` boundary obligations discharge by conversion. The interval/Face value layer (`Dnf`, `canF`, …) is unchanged. `sketch.lp` §5 also carries a `funext` demonstration — the minimal case where the boundary `when` must fire **under a binder** (`λ x`), doubling as the motivating example for type-directed conversion.

# Design conclusion: one judgement, `Θ′ ⊢ a ≡ b` — merge, don't orient

The whole proposal simplifies to a single reframing of conversion, and the rest is engineering scoped around it.

**The judgement.** Conversion becomes `Θ′ ⊢ a ≡ b`, where `Θ′` is a set of equations carried as a congruence-closure structure — an **e-graph**. The `theory` layer canonizes interval/face terms into `Dnf` (Shostak canonizer, no e-graph needed for the algebra itself); the `when` layer **merges** its head equations into `Θ′` (boundary `p @ i0 = u`, path-η, the hcomp/comp face laws, primPOr overlap-agreement) instead of orienting them as rewrites. "Decide `a ≡ b`" = "are `a`, `b` in the same class after congruence closure of `Θ′`".

**Why merge and not orient — the confluence point, settled.** Path-η and overlap-agreement have no canonical orientation; forcing them into directed rewrite rules is exactly the non-confluence the analysis kept hitting. Union-and-congruence-close sidesteps it: an equivalence has no critical pairs to converge, and the congruence closure of a set of equations is **unique regardless of merge order** (the egglog/Kleene-fixpoint guarantee), so the order-dependence worry disappears by construction. The e-graph is therefore not a heavy add-on we reach for reluctantly — it is the *minimal* structure that lets us stop fighting confluence. `Θ′` **is** the e-graph; `Φ_Γ` is its cofibration slice.

**The ground/metavariable split — this is the simplification.**
- **Ground** (all of §1–§6, the four-corner squares, `funext`): matching the `$`-pattern `when` schemas against a ground subject instantiates every slot to a ground term, so only ground equations enter `Θ′`. Ground congruence closure is finite, order-independent, **decidable**. This is the deliverable, and it is the formal system the CCHM metatheory is actually about (the core calculus has no metavariables).
- **Metavariable** (a subject still carrying `?`): merging an equation can *solve* a metavariable, so saturation interleaves with unification = **E-unification**. This is the one open/undecidable-in-general case (Metatheory §3), and it is the *only* thing the thesis leaves open.

**Kernel discipline (Lean's lesson).** Keep conversion **ground-only** and decidable in the trusted core; push metavariable solving out to the **elaborator**, using ordinary pattern (Miller) unification — *not* modulo the theory. When a metavariable can only be pinned by a theory equation, postpone or require an annotation rather than run theory E-unification in the kernel. The kernel then only ever sees ground `Θ′`. The cost is honest and measurable: occasionally the user must write an endpoint/face that fuller E-unification would have inferred — partly re-admitting the `@u₀,u₁` annotation line 4 set out to drop. Build the ground e-graph first (it covers everything demonstrated); scope theory-aware elaboration as a clearly-delimited extension whose termination is the stated open problem.
